Donghyeok Park

Robotics & Physical AI for Aerospace Manufacturing

I am a robotics engineer working in aerospace manufacturing, focusing on the deployment of humanoid and collaborative robotic systems in industrial environments.

My interests lie in improving the repeatability, robustness, and autonomy of robotic systems operating under real-world production constraints. In particular, I am interested in how robots can reliably perceive complex environments and perform manipulation tasks in high-precision manufacturing settings.

I received my M.S. in Artificial Intelligence in 2024 and my B.S. in Artificial Intelligence and Electrical Engineering. I currently work at Korea Aerospace Industries (KAI), the only aircraft system integrator in South Korea, where I contribute to robotics system integration and automation in aerospace manufacturing.

I am interested in pursuing a PhD in robotics to study how robotic systems can achieve reliable autonomy and industrial-level robustness in real-world environments.

Publications

Practical Adversarial Attacks Imperceptible to Humans in Visual Recognition

Donghyeok Park*, Sumin Yeon, Hyeon Seo, Seok-Jun Bu, and Suwon Lee.
Computer Modeling in Engineering & Sciences(SCI-indexed), 2025
[ Paper ]

This paper proposes a practical black-box adversarial attack that generates image perturbations imperceptible to humans while successfully fooling visual recognition models. Unlike prior work assuming white-box access or unlimited queries, the attack operates under realistic constraints with limited model access and query budgets. Experimental results show high attack success rates and strong human imperceptibility, highlighting real-world security risks in deep learning systems.

Cobot Path Optimization Considering Obstacles, Vision and Joint Velocity Constraints

Donghyeok Park*, Changyeon Kim, Jeongun Lee, and Hunok Choi
Accepted for presentation at the Korean Society for Aeronautical and Space Sciences (KSAS) Spring Conference, 2026.
[Preprint available upon request]

This paper presents a task path optimization method for collaborative robots considering obstacles, vision constraints, and heterogeneous joint velocities. A unified joint-space time cost function is formulated to minimize overall cycle time. Experimental comparisons demonstrate improved efficiency over conventional path planning strategies.

Research Projects

Humanoid Logistics Automation in Aerospace Production Lines

Korean Government-Funded Humanoid Robotics Project
AUG 2025 - MAR 2026

Developed a Physical AI framework for humanoid-driven logistics (RB-Y1) in an active aerospace manufacturing environment. The system integrated 2D LiDAR-based SLAM for precise navigation and YOLOv8-based perception for real-time part verification, enabling safe handling of aerospace components.

My Contributions

  • Designed and implemented navigation strategies using LiDAR-based SLAM for inter-facility logistics
  • Integrated YOLOv8-based perception to verify part presence before manipulation tasks
  • Developed perception-driven task execution logic for autonomous decision-making
  • Implemented teleoperation-based imitation learning for box picking and loading sequences

Results
Achieved >96% success rate in autonomous logistics tasks over 31 cumulative hours of real-world operation.

Technologies: Python, YOLOv8, 2D LiDAR SLAM, RGB Vision Systems, Humanoid Robot (RB-Y1)

Collaborative Robot Drilling Automation for Aircraft Wing Assembly

Korean Government-Funded Aerospace Manufacturing Automation Project
JUL 2025 - FEB 2026

This project investigated the feasibility of automating aircraft wing drilling operations using a collaborative robot integrated with a vision-based hole localization system. The robot identified drilling positions using machine vision and aligned its end-effector orientation before triggering drilling operations via PLC-based control signals.

My Contributions

  • Implemented vision-based hole detection workflow using MechVision
  • Computed orientation corrections (Rx, Ry, Rz) for accurate drilling alignment
  • Integrated robotic motion execution with PLC-based drilling control signals
  • Developed Python-based logic connecting vision outputs to robot motion commands

Technologies: Python, MechVision, Industrial PLC Integration, Collaborative Robot
